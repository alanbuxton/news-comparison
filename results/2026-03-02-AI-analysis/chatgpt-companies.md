# 1️⃣ Tavily

**Volume:** 1,940 results (very high)
**Signal-to-noise ratio:** Low

## Strengths

* Extremely comprehensive coverage.
* Captures a wide range of financial, investment, and market content.
* Strong at surfacing:

  * Earnings updates
  * Analyst commentary
  * Market positioning signals
  * Investor sentiment

## Weaknesses

* **Very noisy**.
* Many results are:

  * Stock roundups (“What to know before the bell…”)
  * Generic market commentary
  * Finance portal summaries
  * Automated stock coverage pages
* High number of near-duplicate finance articles.
* Often not genuinely about company activity (more about share price movements).
* Many finance sources likely sit behind soft paywalls.
* Requires heavy manual filtering.

## Assessment for your use case

If you're preparing for a strategic meeting, Tavily gives too much noise. You would need to click through many irrelevant or shallow pieces to extract meaningful developments like M&A, expansion, regulatory shifts, or executive moves.

**Verdict:** Too noisy for executive prep without significant filtering.

# 2️⃣ Exa

**Volume:** 926 results
**Signal-to-noise ratio:** Moderate to low

## Strengths

* Broad source diversity (press releases, energy media, national press).
* Surfaces legitimate articles including:

  * Expansion projects
  * Investment announcements
  * Strategic evaluations
  * Government-related developments involving companies
* Good at catching mid-tier trade publications.

## Weaknesses

* Large number of indirect mentions.

  * Example pattern: macro oil price articles where ExxonMobil is mentioned in passing.
* Many press-release style articles.
* Some articles not primarily about the company.
* Requires filtering to extract core activity.
* Some regional/industry news sites with uneven summary depth.

## Assessment for your use case

Exa is strong on **breadth**, but weaker on precision. You would still need to manually determine which stories are genuinely strategic vs. contextual industry background.

**Verdict:** Good raw feed, but not ideal if you want clean executive-level briefing material quickly.

# 3️⃣ Linkup

**Volume:** 122 results
**Signal-to-noise ratio:** Good

## Strengths

* Much more targeted.
* Higher proportion of articles where:

  * The company is central to the story.
  * There is a clear corporate action (financials, investments, legal matters, expansion).
* Lower volume means easier review.
* Fewer stock ticker roundup articles.
* Good balance between press releases and reporting.

## Weaknesses

* Lower coverage breadth compared to Exa and Tavily.
* May miss some secondary trade or regional publications.
* Summaries are sometimes brief.

## Assessment for your use case

Linkup gives a manageable set of relevant business developments. It avoids much of the stock-noise problem seen in Tavily.

**Verdict:** Strong practical option for business meeting prep.
